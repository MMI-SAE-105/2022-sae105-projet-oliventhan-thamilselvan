Methagu, the most contreversial biopic

Methagu is a biographical film of LTTE leader Prabhakaran. It was directed by T.Kittu and released in 2021. The movie had an approximative budget of 60 lakhs Indian ruppees and it was produced by Riyaz. Actor Kutti Manni was chosen to play Prabhakaran, while Lizzie Antony played Sirimavo Bandaranaike, the former Prime Minister of Sri Lanka. Praveen Kumar is the composer of the biopic. The film was supposed to be released on November 26, 2020, but due to the COVID-19 pandemic, it was postponed and finally released on June 25, 2021



The birth of a legend

The first part of the film deals with the early stage of Prabhakaran's life and ends with Prabhakaran killing the governor of Jaffna. The film tells the story of Prabhakaran in his early years, what events influenced him and how he built the liberation movement while clearly explaining the politics behind the ongoing war in Sri Lanka. In this movie directed by Kittu, a short film director, Mani Prabhakaran, who is playing the role of Prabhakaran, has performed so well that everything unfolds before the eyes of the fans. The film clearly talks about the Tamil Research Conference held in Sri Lanka and the Cold War between Sri Lankan President Bandaranaike and Selva.

A directional masterclass

It is the first film of the director and he producer. They don’t have any filmography.

Although the film is made on a very low budget, the elegance shown by the crew in technology conveys the feeling of watching a good movie to the audience. It has tried to tell the story to the fans in the form of street art, which is a novel experience for many moviegoers. As the film is talking about Sri Lankan politics, this film is an attempt to create understanding about the Sri Lankan war without causing any uproar in the Indian Union. Fans are praising the film as Medagu will be a good guide for the next generation trying to understand the Sri Lankan war. The movie has been released on pay-per-view mode on the BS value website.
